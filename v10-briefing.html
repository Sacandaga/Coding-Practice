II · Executive SummaryCJCS to POTUS

Mr. President —

The United States is fifteen days into the most significant military engagement since the 2003 invasion of Iraq. The February 28 joint US–Israel strike campaign killed Supreme Leader Khamenei and decapitated much of the senior IRGC command. Iran has responded with over 500 ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones against U.S. regional installations and Israel. The Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed. GCC allies are absorbing daily attacks. Oil prices have surged 40%+. Campaign costs approach $1.5–1.9 billion per day with no congressional appropriation in place.

Three decisions are before you Monday. Iran theater: the Joint Chiefs assess that a managed exit — sustained military pressure paired with diplomatic re-engagement via Oman — offers the highest probability of a favorable endstate while avoiding catastrophic escalation. Houthi reentry: 40–50% probability of limited Red Sea attacks within 30 days; pre-positioning for Operation Rough Rider II is recommended immediately. Hormuz reopening: escort operations should begin within 72 hours — every day of closure costs the global economy $1.5–2 billion in disrupted trade.

The Joint Chiefs assess the most likely endstate (35%) is a protracted stalemate exceeding 60 days at a cost above $50 billion absent diplomatic intervention. The recommended course of action combines Hormuz escorts, Houthi deterrence signaling, and reactivation of the Omani diplomatic channel.

VII · Houthi Reentry CalculusDay 15 assessment · 40–50% in 30 days

Factors favoring restraint

  • Decapitation trauma — Israel killed Houthi PM, ~12 cabinet members, missile/drone chief Zakaria Hajar (Aug–Sep 2025). Institutional memory deters provocation.
  • Arsenal degradation — CENTCOM seized 750+ tons of Iranian materiel (Jul 2025). 80%+ were manufacturing inputs, not finished weapons.
  • Saudi détente at risk — 2022 truce with Riyadh is the Houthis' most valuable diplomatic asset.
  • Threat-as-strategy — Restraint-with-threat already keeps insurance premiums elevated and Saudi planning fixated on a southern front.
  • 2025 ceasefire gains — Trump halted strikes in May 2025. Resuming attacks forfeits this de-escalation gain.

Factors favoring reentry

  • Ideological imperative — Abdul-Malik al-Houthi has explicitly stated "hands are on the trigger." Failure to act risks credibility.
  • Iranian activation request — Mojtaba Khamenei publicly invoked the Axis of Resistance.
  • Coalition cohesion — Acting in solidarity reinforces the proxy network's coherence.
  • U.S. distraction — CENTCOM assets fully committed to Iran theater. Operational window is open.
JCS assessmentThe Houthis are hedging. The most likely path is symbolic / limited strikes intended to satisfy ideological commitments without triggering Operation Rough Rider II. Pre-positioning for renewed operations should begin Monday regardless of the political track.
